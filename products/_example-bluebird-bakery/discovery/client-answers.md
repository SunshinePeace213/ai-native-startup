## 1. Business & goals

_Understanding what Bluebird Bakery does and what this website must achieve._

- **What does Bluebird Bakery do, and who do you serve today — walk-in neighborhood customers, event and wedding orders, wholesale accounts, or a mix?**
  _Why we ask: it anchors every later decision to your actual business._
  > We're a neighborhood bakery, been open 8 years now. We do sourdough bread daily — that's kind of our thing, my starter is as old as the shop — plus pastries and coffee for the regulars who come in every morning. And then separately we do custom celebration cakes: birthdays, weddings, baby showers, that kind of thing. No wholesale, no restaurant supplying, nothing like that. So really it's two groups: the regulars who just walk in, and the cake customers who plan ahead.

- **What is the single most important thing this website must accomplish in its first six months — for example, more custom cake inquiries, more walk-in traffic, or fewer phone calls asking for hours and directions?**
  _Why we ask: it forces one primary goal to prioritize scope against._
  > Honestly, the cake inquiries. Right now people message my Instagram asking about a cake for their kid's birthday or whatever, and it just gets buried under everything else — comments, other DMs, my part-timer Jenna posting photos. I've definitely lost orders because I didn't see the message in time. I want a real form so those don't slip through the cracks anymore.

- **How will you know the website worked — more orders, more foot traffic, more catering inquiries, or something else?**
  _Why we ask: it turns "a website" into a measurable business outcome._
  > More cake orders coming in through an actual form instead of getting lost in DMs, I guess — and not hearing "oh I tried to message you and never heard back" anymore. I couldn't put a number on it though (we get into that more later, I know).

- **Is this a brand-new website, a redesign of an existing site, or a replacement for something that isn't working for you today (an outdated site, or relying on a Facebook or Instagram page)?**
  _Why we ask: it sets baseline expectations and migration risk._
  > Brand new — we don't have a website at all right now. We've just got our Google Business listing and the Instagram. The Instagram is doing double duty as our "website" and it's really not built for that, especially for the cake orders.

## 2. Audience & users

_Who this site is for and what each visitor needs to get done._

- **Who are the two or three main types of people who'll visit this site — for example, neighborhood regulars checking hours, someone planning a birthday or wedding and looking for a custom cake, or a business ordering pastries for an office — and what is each one trying to do?**
  _Why we ask: these personas drive the site's structure, copy, and features._
  > Three, I'd say. One: regulars who just want to know we're open, what's fresh today, and where to park. Two: someone planning a birthday, wedding, or shower who wants a custom cake and needs to figure out how to actually order one from us. Three, kind of new to me but it happens — people who just moved to the neighborhood or are visiting and find us on Google and want to know what we're about before they walk in.

- **Will most of your customers be checking the site on their phone (on the way to the shop, deciding where to stop) or on a desktop computer (planning a bigger order in advance)?**
  _Why we ask: it sets the mobile-versus-desktop priority._
  > Phone, mostly, I'd bet — that's how everyone finds us on Google already. Though I could see someone sitting down at their computer to fill out a whole cake order with all the details, so it should work fine on a computer too. But design for the phone first.

- **What is the one action you most want each visitor to take — call the bakery, get directions and hours, submit a custom order or catering request, or something else?**
  _Why we ask: it defines the primary path we design toward._
  > If I had to pick just one, it's the custom cake form — that's the thing I actually need fixed. Hours and directions matter too, obviously, but people can already get those from Google. The cake inquiries are the ones we're losing.

- **Is your customer base mostly local to your neighborhood, or do you also want the site to reach visitors, tourists, or customers who'd prefer information in a language other than English?**
  _Why we ask: it flags localization and language needs early._
  > Mostly local — our neighborhood, plus cake customers who'll drive across town for a special order once they hear about us. Everybody I deal with is fine in English, so no need for other languages that I know of.

## 5. Structure & pages

- **What are the must-have pages for launch — for example Home, Menu, About/Our Story, Location & Hours, Order/Catering, Contact?**
  _Why we ask: it defines the minimum footprint we build._
  > I'd say: Home, Menu, Our Story, Custom Cakes, and a Visit Us / Contact page with our hours and location. Those five feel like the whole thing to me.

- **How do you picture someone moving from the homepage to the action you want — browsing the menu first, or going straight to a contact or order form?**
  _Why we ask: it surfaces the primary flow we should design for._
  > For the cake customers, I want the homepage to point them straight at the Custom Cakes page and then the inquiry form — don't make them dig. For the regulars, hours and location should be easy to spot right away too, since that's what most people actually want in a hurry.

- **Are there sections you're unsure about that we should check with you before building — a full online store, a blog, a loyalty page?**
  _Why we ask: it flags soft scope worth confirming before we commit to it._

The one thing I'm not sure about is whether a photo gallery of past cakes should be its own page or just part of the Custom Cakes page. Leaning toward just putting it on the Custom Cakes page, but happy to hear what you think.

- **Do you have an existing website, or a Facebook/Instagram page acting as one, whose structure we should keep or deliberately break from?**
  _Why we ask: it manages migration and sets customer-habit expectations._
  > The Instagram is basically our stand-in website right now, and honestly the thing I want to break from is the "just DM me" way of doing cake orders. That's the whole problem. Keep the photos-of-cakes vibe if you want, but the ordering-by-DM part needs to go.

## 6. Features & functionality

_What the site needs to do beyond a menu and photos._

- **Beyond static content, what must the site do — online ordering for pickup or delivery, a custom cake or catering inquiry form, gift card purchases, a newsletter signup, a loyalty program?**
  _Why we ask: it separates a brochure site from something closer to an application, which changes cost and timeline significantly._
  > The one thing I actually need is a custom cake inquiry form. That's it, that's the must-have. No online ordering for pickup or delivery — we're not set up for that and don't want to be right now. No gift cards, no loyalty program, no newsletter, at least not for launch.

- **For each of those, is it required for launch, or something we can plan for a later phase?**
  _Why we ask: it keeps the first release focused and on budget._
  > Cake inquiry form: required, launch. Everything else — online ordering, gift cards, newsletter, loyalty program — later, maybe, not for launch. Maybe never, we'll see.

- **Have you seen features on other bakery or food-business sites that you consider essential for yours?**
  _Why we ask: it grounds expectations in concrete references rather than abstract wishes._
  > Not really — I'm not great at browsing around other sites and picking out features, if I'm honest. I just know the cake form is the thing that matters.

- **What should happen when someone submits a custom order or catering inquiry — should it email you directly, should the customer get an automatic confirmation, does it need to reach a specific staff member?**
  _Why we ask: it defines the success and failure states of your most important form._
  > It should come straight to my email — I'm the one who handles all the cake orders, nobody else needs to be looped in. And I really like the idea of the customer getting some kind of automatic "we got it, we'll be in touch" message, since that's exactly the reassurance people aren't getting from Instagram DMs today.

## 11. References & competitors

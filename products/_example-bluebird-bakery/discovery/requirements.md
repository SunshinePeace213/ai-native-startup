# Locked Requirements — Bluebird Bakery

The frozen discovery record. Each of the twelve dimensions carries a marker line of the
exact shape `- <dimension>: <state>` where `<state>` is:

- `open` — still gathering; not yet safe to build against.
- `locked` — the dimension's "locked when" criterion (see the cpo-question-bank skill) is
  met; the facts below are frozen and the PRD may cite them.

When a dimension locks, change its marker `<state>` to `locked` (for example
`- business & goals: locked`) and fill its content block with the agreed facts. Late
client answers that contradict a locked dimension re-open it and mark downstream stages
`stale`.

## business & goals

- business & goals: locked

- Business: Neighborhood bakery, open 8 years. Two customer groups: walk-in regulars
  (daily sourdough, pastries, coffee) and custom celebration-cake customers (birthdays,
  weddings, baby showers). No wholesale or restaurant supply.
- Primary goal: Stop losing custom cake orders to buried Instagram DMs — give customers
  a real inquiry form so requests stop slipping through the cracks.
- Top outcomes: (1) more cake orders arriving through the form instead of DMs; (2) an
  end to "I messaged and never heard back" complaints.
- Build type: New build — Bluebird Bakery has no website today; its only online presence
  is a Google Business listing and Instagram.

## audience & users

- audience & users: locked

- Primary user types:
  1. Neighborhood regulars — want today's hours, what's fresh, and parking.
  2. Cake customers (birthday / wedding / baby shower) — want to work out how to place
     a custom cake order.
  3. New or visiting people who find the bakery via Google and want to know what it's
     about before walking in.
- Primary desired action: submit the custom cake inquiry form — named explicitly as the
  one action that matters most; hours/directions are secondary since Google already
  covers those.
- Device priority: design mobile-first (most discovery happens via phone/Google); must
  also work well on desktop for customers filling in a full cake order in advance.
- Locale / languages: mostly local neighborhood plus cake customers willing to travel
  across town; English only, no other languages needed.

## brand & voice

- brand & voice: locked

- Brand assets: colors defined — robin's-egg blue and cream, used since opening. Logo
  exists only hand-painted on the physical shop sign; **gap** — no digital file exists
  and it likely needs to be recreated as a usable asset.
- Desired tone: warm, homey, welcoming — explicitly not fancy or upscale.
- Must-use messaging: none verbatim; "since 2018" (founding year) may be used if useful.
  Copy is to be agency-drafted — client review is for tone/accuracy, not word-for-word
  control (see content & assets).
- Aesthetic direction: simple, uncluttered — expanded under references & competitors.

## content & assets

- content & assets: locked

- Content inventory: menu currently exists only as an in-shop chalkboard — client will
  provide a cleaned-up version. No formal ingredient/allergen list exists today (gap —
  tracked under legal/compliance). Professional photography already exists (sourdough
  loaves, cakes, shop interior, shot by the client's photographer friend last spring) —
  client owns the files and will supply them; no stock imagery needed. Logo exists only
  on the physical shop sign (see brand & voice gap).
- Copy responsibility: Soriza drafts all site copy; Maria reviews and gives final
  approval before anything goes live.
- Post-launch updates: content changes often — daily specials plus a handful of
  seasonal/holiday menu shifts per year. Client wants this mostly hands-off for her team;
  delegated to agency — recommend a lightweight CMS for simple edits (hours, specials)
  that part-timer Jenna could optionally use, with the agency retaining primary update
  responsibility.

## structure & pages

- structure & pages: locked

- Launch pages/screens: Home, Menu, Our Story, Custom Cakes, Visit Us / Contact (hours
  and location).
- Primary navigation path: the homepage routes cake customers straight to the Custom
  Cakes page and its inquiry form without making them dig; hours and location stay
  immediately visible for regulars.
- Delegated decision: whether a past-cakes photo gallery is its own page or a section of
  the Custom Cakes page is undecided. Client leans toward folding it into the Custom
  Cakes page and explicitly delegated the final call to the agency.

## features & functionality

- features & functionality: locked

- Launch features: a custom cake/catering inquiry form — the only feature required for
  launch.
- Later / deferred (not for launch, possibly never): online ordering for pickup or
  delivery, gift cards, a loyalty program, newsletter signup.
- Key-action outcome: form submission emails Maria directly (sole recipient — she
  handles all cake orders); the customer receives an automatic "we got it, we'll be in
  touch" confirmation.

## integrations & data

- integrations & data: locked

- Required integrations: none required for launch. Square POS is in-store checkout only
  — no site connection needed. Google Business Profile — link out only, for
  reviews/directions. Instagram feed embed is a nice-to-have, not a requirement. No
  online-ordering platform or email-marketing tool needed.
- Data destinations: cake inquiry form submissions go to Maria's email inbox only — no
  spreadsheet, no POS integration.
- Account ownership: all relevant accounts (Square, Google Business Profile, Instagram)
  already exist; nothing new needs provisioning.
- Migration source: none — no customer email list or menu spreadsheet to migrate.
  Existing Instagram photos may be reused as site content but are not a data migration.

## technical & hosting constraints

- technical & hosting constraints: locked

- Domain / hosting: neither exists yet; both must be arranged as part of this project.
- Mandated / forbidden tech: no required or forbidden platform — delegated to the
  agency's judgment. Hard constraint: the client must never need to touch servers or any
  technical infrastructure herself.
- Maintenance owner: an ongoing agency-managed arrangement after launch. Part-timer
  Jenna may make genuinely simple edits (e.g., hours) if the tooling makes that easy, but
  the agency owns technical upkeep.
- Performance / accessibility standards: no specific standard named by the client —
  delegated to the agency ("I'll trust you all to build it properly and do right by
  that"); recommend WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline for a public-facing food-business site.
  Explicit requirement: fast page loads on mobile.

## budget & timeline

- budget & timeline: locked

- Budget: $6,000–9,000, confirmed with partner Dave.
- Target date: no fixed date or event, but launch before the holiday season.
- Date-vs-scope priority: date wins over scope — launch the core five pages (Home,
  Menu, Our Story, Custom Cakes, Visit Us/Contact) plus the cake inquiry form before
  the holiday season; anything beyond that core is deferred past launch.

## success metrics

- success metrics: locked

- Core metric: cake-inquiry form submissions.
- Baseline: ~10 cake inquiries/month currently arrive via Instagram DM.
- Target: 15+ inquiries/month through the site form.
- Check-ins: one month post-launch, and again after the holiday season.

## references & competitors

- references & competitors: locked

- Competitors: Miller's Hearth Bakehouse (across town, also sourdough-focused) and
  Petal & Crumb Cake Studio (downtown, custom-cake focused).
- Positive references: Miller's Hearth Bakehouse (simple site, large photos, hours
  prominent at the top); Petal & Crumb Cake Studio (their cake inquiry form shows
  examples by size/style before the customer commits — client wants this idea, not the
  exact look, applied to Bluebird's own inquiry form); Fresh Pot Coffee (simple,
  no-frills neighbor site).
- Anti-references: big supermarket-bakery-style sites — stock photography, pop-ups
  pushing signups on arrival; feels impersonal/"robotic."
- Differentiator: everything made from scratch with a sourdough starter as old as the
  bakery itself; custom cakes are hand-decorated by Maria herself — no mixes, no two
  cakes exactly alike.

## legal/compliance

- legal/compliance: locked

- Legal pages: privacy policy and terms of use, drafted by the agency for Maria's
  review and final sign-off — the same approval flow as site copy.
- Regulated data: allergy/dietary notes captured by the cake form are treated as
  sensitive data, visible only to Maria.
- Regulations/certifications: the health permit remains displayed in-store only, not
  on the site.

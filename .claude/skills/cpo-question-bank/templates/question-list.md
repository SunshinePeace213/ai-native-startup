## 1. Business & goals

_Understanding what your business does and what this project must achieve._

- **What does your business do, and who does it serve today?**
  _Why we ask: it anchors every later decision to your actual offering._
  >
- **What is the single most important thing this build must accomplish in its first six months?**
  _Why we ask: it forces one primary goal to prioritize scope against._
  >
- **Is this a new build, a redesign, or a rescue of something that isn't working?**
  _Why we ask: it sets baseline expectations and migration risk._
  >

## 2. Audience & users

_Who this is for and what they need to get done._

- **Who are the two or three main types of people who will use this, and what is each trying to do?**
  _Why we ask: these personas drive structure, copy, and features._
  >
- **What is the one action you most want each visitor to take?**
  _Why we ask: it defines the primary path we design toward._
  >

## 3. Brand & voice

_How the site should look and sound._

- **Do you have brand guidelines, a logo, and a color palette, or do those need to be created?**
  _Why we ask: it separates applying your brand from defining it._
  >
- **In three words, how should the site feel to a first-time visitor?**
  _Why we ask: it gives our designers a concrete tone target._
  >

## 4. Content & assets

_What content exists and who produces it._

- **What content already exists (text, images, video, logos), and who owns it?**
  _Why we ask: it distinguishes what we reuse from what we create._
  >
- **Who will write and approve the words — you or us?**
  _Why we ask: it assigns copy responsibility and affects the timeline._
  >

## 5. Structure & pages

_The shape of the site and how people move through it._

- **What are the must-have pages or screens for launch?**
  _Why we ask: it defines the minimum footprint we build._
  >
- **How do you picture someone moving from landing to the action you want?**
  _Why we ask: it surfaces the primary user flow._
  >

## 6. Features & functionality

_What the site needs to do beyond static content._

- **Beyond static content, what must the site do — forms, booking, search, accounts, payments?**
  _Why we ask: it separates a brochure site from an application._
  >
- **For each of those, is it required for launch or a later phase?**
  _Why we ask: it keeps the first release focused and on budget._
  >

## 7. Integrations & data

_The tools this connects to and where data goes._

- **What third-party tools must this connect to — CRM, email, payments, analytics, scheduling?**
  _Why we ask: integrations carry the biggest hidden technical cost._
  >
- **Where does the data users enter need to end up?**
  _Why we ask: it defines destinations, ownership, and compliance._
  >

## 8. Technical & hosting constraints

_The technical ground rules._

- **Do you have a domain and hosting already, or do those need arranging?**
  _Why we ask: it determines launch logistics and where the site lives._
  >
- **Is there a platform or technology you must use or must avoid?**
  _Why we ask: hard constraints bound the whole solution._
  >

## 9. Budget & timeline

_The time and money we're working within._

- **Is there a hard launch date or event this must be ready for?**
  _Why we ask: a fixed date forces scope tradeoffs._
  >
- **If we had to choose, is hitting the date or the full feature set more important?**
  _Why we ask: it pre-agrees the tradeoff before pressure hits._
  >

## 10. Success metrics

_How we'll know it worked._

- **How will you measure whether this was worth it — what number moves?**
  _Why we ask: it makes success testable rather than a feeling._
  >
- **What is that number today, and where do you want it to be, and by when?**
  _Why we ask: a baseline and target make the metric measurable._
  >

## 11. References & competitors

_Where you sit in your market._

- **Who are your main competitors, and what do their sites do well or badly?**
  _Why we ask: it positions your build in its market._
  >
- **Show us two or three sites you'd love yours to stand beside, and any to avoid.**
  _Why we ask: concrete references beat abstract adjectives._
  >

## 12. Legal & compliance

_The legal and regulatory requirements._

- **Do you need a privacy policy, terms, cookie consent, or accessibility compliance?**
  _Why we ask: legal pages are easy to forget and costly to retrofit._
  >
- **Do you handle any sensitive or regulated data (health, financial, children's)?**
  _Why we ask: regulated data changes the entire data-handling design._
  >
